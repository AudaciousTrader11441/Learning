# AI Engineering Disciplines

## Overview

This document is the entry point to the Learning hub. It covers the **Four Disciplines** of AI input and the **Five Levels** of autonomous software development. The fourth discipline -- Specification Engineering -- serves as the bridge, expanding into the practical realities of dark factories, organisational change, and the PREVC workflow.

## Context

Between October 2025 and February 2026, AI coding agents shifted from conversational assistants to autonomous workers capable of running for hours without human input. The prompting skill that mattered in 2024 -- crafting clever instructions inside a chat window -- hit a ceiling. What replaced it is a stack of four disciplines, each operating at a different altitude. Mastering the stack determines whether an organisation rides the wave or drowns in almost-right output.

The content is organised into three roles. Each role's document tells the same story from a different vantage point, in question-and-answer form. Read the section that matches your role, or read all three to see the full picture.

| Role            | Who they are                                                                     | What they need                                                                                |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Soldier**     | The technical practitioner writing specs, building context, and directing agents | Deep technical understanding of each discipline, practical primitives, and workflow mechanics |
| **Captain**     | The team lead guiding soldiers through adoption                                  | How to assess, sequence, and manage the transition; how the org chart shifts                  |
| **Counsellor**  | The strategic advisor shaping long-term AI capability                            | Where AI fits in the operating model, what competency looks like, and how to align adoption with organisational intent |
| **General**     | The executive responsible for the entire operation                               | Why this matters, what the numbers say, and what the new organisation looks like              |

> This is not a FAQ. A FAQ answers isolated, frequently repeated questions. These guides dissect the same body of knowledge through three lenses, with each Q&A building on the previous one to tell a sequential story for that role.
